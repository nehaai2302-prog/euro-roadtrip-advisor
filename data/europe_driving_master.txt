# MASTER KNOWLEDGE BASE: EUROPEAN ROAD LOGISTICS 2026

## REGION: WESTERN EUROPE (France, Belgium, Netherlands)
- TOLLS: France uses 'Péage' booths (barrier system). Use 'Télépéage' lanes for tags like Liber-t.
- ZONES: Paris and Lyon require 'Crit'Air' stickers. Fines for missing stickers start at €68.
- EQUIPMENT: 1 reflective vest per occupant (stored in cabin) and 1 warning triangle are mandatory.

## REGION: ALPINE COUNTRIES (Switzerland, Austria)
- HIGHWAYS: Switzerland requires a physical or e-vignette (40 CHF). Austria (Vienna and all motorway driving): vignette required on Autobahnen; separate section tolls apply on some corridors in addition to the vignette. Confirm current tariffs on https://www.asfinag.at/

- WINTER: From Nov 1 to April 15, winter tires are mandatory. Snow chains must be in the trunk if driving in mountain passes.
- LIGHTS: Daytime running lights are mandatory year-round in Switzerland.

## REGION: CENTRAL EUROPE (Germany, Poland, Czech Republic)
- AUTOBAHN: Germany is free for cars, but Poland and Czech Republic use vignettes or electronic tolling (e-TOLL).
- EQUIPMENT: Germany requires a First Aid kit (DIN 13164 standard) and a warning triangle.
- ZONES: German cities require 'Umweltzone' (Green Stickers).

## REGION: MEDITERRANEAN (Italy, Spain, Portugal)
- ITALY: Avoid ZTL (Zona Traffico Limitato) centers; they are for locals only and cameras will fine you automatically.
- SPAIN: V16 beacons (emergency flashing lights) have replaced warning triangles as of Jan 2026.
- PARKING: Blue lines mean paid parking; white lines are free; yellow is restricted.

## GENERAL CROSS-BORDER RULES
- LICENSE: A standard EU/UK license is valid. US/Australian drivers should carry an International Driving Permit (IDP).
- BLOOD ALCOHOL: The standard limit is 0.05% (0.02% in Norway/Poland). Zero tolerance for novice drivers.
- PHONES: Using a handheld phone is illegal everywhere. Fines in the Netherlands can reach €400+.

## REGION: BALTIC STATES (Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania)
HIGHWAYS: No tolls for passenger cars (as of current regulations).
SPEED LIMITS:
-Highways: 90–110 km/h
-Urban: 50 km/h
LIGHTS: 
-Headlights must be ON at all times (day and night).
ALCOHOL LIMIT:
-Estonia: 0.02%
-Latvia & Lithuania: 0.05% (0.02% for new drivers)
WINTER: Winter tires are mandatory (typically Nov–March).
SAFETY: Reflective vest required if exiting vehicle on road.

## REGION: UNITED KINGDOM (England, Scotland, Wales)
DRIVING SIDE: Left-hand driving.
SPEED LIMITS:
Motorways: 70 mph (~112 km/h)
Rural roads: 60 mph
Urban areas: 20–30 mph
TOLLS: Most roads are free. Exceptions include:
Dartford Crossing (electronic toll)
CONGESTION CHARGES:
London has congestion and ULEZ zones (daily charges apply).
FUEL: Petrol stations are frequent but expensive compared to mainland Europe.

## GENERAL CROSS-BORDER RULES
LICENSE: EU licenses are valid across the UK and Baltics. Non-EU drivers (US, Australia, etc.) should carry an International Driving Permit (IDP).
DRIVING SIDE: UK drives on the LEFT. Most of Europe (including Baltics) drives on the RIGHT.
DOCUMENTS: Always carry passport, vehicle registration, and insurance (Green Card recommended for cross-border travel).
SAFETY: Reflective vest and warning triangle are required in most EU countries (mandatory in Baltics).
PHONES: Using a handheld phone while driving is illegal in all regions.

## REGION: DRIVING BETWEEN UK & EUROPE (IMPORTANT)
VEHICLE REQUIREMENTS:
UK cars driving in EU need GB/UK sticker
Headlight beam deflectors required for UK cars in Europe
INSURANCE:
Check cross-border coverage
BREAKS:
Driving + ferry combinations require planning rest periods

## SPECIAL TIPS FOR ROAD TRIPS
PLAN FUEL: Remote areas (Scotland, Baltics) have fewer stations.
NAVIGATION: Offline maps recommended (signal can be weak).
WEATHER: Always check conditions before long drives.
PARKING APPS: Useful in UK cities.

## FERRY TRAVEL (UK ↔ EUROPE & BALTICS)

COMMON ROUTES:
UK ↔ France (Dover–Calais)
UK ↔ Netherlands (Harwich–Hook of Holland)
Germany ↔ Lithuania / Latvia
Sweden ↔ Estonia / Latvia

BOOKING: Advance booking recommended, especially in summer.

VEHICLE CHECK-IN:
Arrive 60–90 minutes before departure
Passport and ticket required

COST:
Based on vehicle size + passengers
Typically €50–€150 per crossing

ONBOARD:
You cannot access your car during the journey
Cabins available on long routes

RULES:
Turn off alarms before boarding
Follow crew instructions for parking
