# MASTER KNOWLEDGE BASE: EUROPEAN ROAD LOGISTICS 2026

## REGION: WESTERN EUROPE (France, Belgium, Netherlands)
- TOLLS: France uses 'Péage' booths (barrier system). Use 'Télépéage' lanes for tags like Liber-t.
- ZONES: Paris and Lyon require 'Crit'Air' stickers. Fines for missing stickers start at €68.
- EQUIPMENT: 1 reflective vest per occupant (stored in cabin) and 1 warning triangle are mandatory.

## REGION: ALPINE COUNTRIES (Switzerland, Austria)
- HIGHWAYS: Switzerland requires a physical or e-vignette (40 CHF). Austria requires a 1-day, 10-day, or annual vignette.
- WINTER: From Nov 1 to April 15, winter tires are mandatory. Snow chains must be in the trunk if driving in mountain passes.
- LIGHTS: Daytime running lights are mandatory year-round in Switzerland.

## REGION: CENTRAL EUROPE (Germany, Poland, Czech Republic)
- AUTOBAHN: Germany is free for cars, but Poland and Czech Republic use vignettes or electronic tolling (e-TOLL).
- EQUIPMENT: Germany requires a First Aid kit (DIN 13164 standard) and a warning triangle.
- ZONES: German cities require 'Umweltzone' (Green Stickers).

## REGION: MEDITERRANEAN (Italy, Spain, Portugal)
- ITALY: Avoid ZTL (Zona Traffico Limitato) centers; they are for locals only and cameras will fine you automatically.
- SPAIN: V16 beacons (emergency flashing lights) have replaced warning triangles as of Jan 2026.
- PARKING: Blue lines mean paid parking; white lines are free; yellow is restricted.

## GENERAL CROSS-BORDER RULES
- LICENSE: A standard EU/UK license is valid. US/Australian drivers should carry an International Driving Permit (IDP).
- BLOOD ALCOHOL: The standard limit is 0.05% (0.02% in Norway/Poland). Zero tolerance for novice drivers.
- PHONES: Using a handheld phone is illegal everywhere. Fines in the Netherlands can reach €400+.
