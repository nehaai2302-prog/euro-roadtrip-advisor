# KNOWLEDGE BASE: TRAVELLER TYPE SPECIFIC ADVICE

## CATEGORY: FAMILIES WITH KIDS
- EQUIPMENT: In Germany and Ireland, booster seats are mandatory for children up to 12 years or 150cm.
- SEATING: Children under 12 are generally prohibited from sitting in the front seat across the EU.
- STOPOVERS: Prioritize "Rasthof" (rest stops) in Germany/Austria as they feature "Sanifair" clean bathrooms and play areas.
- PLANNING: Schedule driving chunks around nap times. Pack a "Pit Stop Kit" with wet wipes, snacks, and a portable potty for toddlers.

## CATEGORY: ELDERLY PARENTS
- ACCESSIBILITY: Avoid historic centers with heavy cobblestones (e.g., Old Town Prague or Rome) if mobility is limited. Use "Park & Ride" services to take accessible trams into the city.
- PACE: Limit driving to a maximum of 3-4 hours per day to prevent fatigue and DVT. 
- COMFORT: Choose vehicles with high seating (SUVs/Crossovers) for easier entry and exit. Ensure the AC is dual-zone to accommodate different temperature preferences.
- STOPOVERS: Focus on thermal spas (e.g., Baden-Baden) or scenic viewpoints that are accessible by car.

## CATEGORY: ROMANTIC COUPLE GATEWAYS
- SCENIC ROUTES: Prioritize the "Romantic Road" in Germany or the "Route des Vins" in Alsace over highways.
- DINING: Look for "Agriturismo" in Italy or "Chateaux" stays in France for secluded, authentic experiences.
- PLANNING: Build in "unstructured time" for wandering narrow alleys in towns like Colmar or Venice.
- TIP: Use smaller cars (e.g., Fiat 500) to easily navigate and park in narrow, romantic Mediterranean streets.

## CATEGORY: TRIPS WITH FRIENDS
- LOGISTICS: Use the "One Captain" rule—one person handles navigation while the other drives.
- BUDGET: Recommend using the 'Splitwise' app for toll and fuel shared costs.
- ENTERTAINMENT: Download offline Spotify playlists; radio signal can be weak in the Alps or rural France.
- EQUIPMENT: Ensure the car has a high-speed multi-port USB charger. Avoid overpacking the trunk so the rear-view mirror isn't blocked.

## CATEGORY: SOLO TRAVELERS
- SAFETY: Never post your location on social media in real-time. Only drive during daylight hours.
- PARKING: Always reverse into parking spots at night for a quick exit if you feel uncomfortable.
- COMMUNICATION: Share a "Live Location" link or a Google Doc of your itinerary with an emergency contact.
- GEAR: Carry a physical whistle and an external power bank in your "day bag."
