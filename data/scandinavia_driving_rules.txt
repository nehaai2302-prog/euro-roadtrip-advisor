🇸🇨 NORDIC DRIVING GUIDE: NORWAY, SWEDEN, DENMARK
SECTION 1: TOLLS & BRIDGES
NORWAY (AutoPASS): Norway uses an almost entirely automatic toll system. Most rental cars have an AutoPASS tag. If driving your own car, you should register your plate at EPASS24. There are also "Studded Tire Fees" in cities like Oslo and Bergen during winter.

SWEDEN: Generally, Swedish motorways are free. However, Stockholm and Gothenburg have "Congestion Taxes" (Trängselskatt) which are automatically billed via license plate cameras.

DENMARK: Motorways are free, but the two major bridges—The Great Belt (Storebælt) and The Øresund Bridge (connecting to Sweden)—have high tolls (approx. €35–€50 per crossing).

FERRIES: In Norway, ferries are considered part of the road network. Most use the "AutoPASS for ferry" system for automatic billing.

SECTION 2: MANDATORY EQUIPMENT
ALL COUNTRIES: You must drive with dipped headlights (low beams) 24 hours a day, even in bright sunshine.

NORWAY: You must carry at least one reflective safety vest and a warning triangle. It is highly recommended to carry a shovel and tow rope in winter.

SWEDEN: Reflective vest and warning triangle are mandatory. If driving in winter, you must carry a shovel and anti-freeze screen wash.

DENMARK: Warning triangle is mandatory. Fire extinguishers and first aid kits are recommended but not legally required for private cars.

SECTION 3: WINTER RULES (CRITICAL)
NORWAY: - Dates: Nov 1 to first Sunday after Easter.

Tires: Winter tires are mandatory in winter conditions. Minimum tread depth: 3mm (5mm recommended).

Studs: Studded tires (Piggdekk) are legal during the winter dates but require a daily permit in major cities.

SWEDEN: - Dates: Dec 1 to March 31.

Requirement: Mandatory to use winter tires if there is snow, ice, or slush on the road.

Heavy Vehicles: Starting 2026, heavy vehicles must use winter tires from Nov 10 to April 10 regardless of weather.

DENMARK: - Requirement: No strict "date-based" law, but from July 2025, it is illegal to drive with "unsuitable" tires in winter conditions. If it snows and you have summer tires, you will be fined.
